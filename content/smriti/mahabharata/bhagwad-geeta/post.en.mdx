# The Song of the Lord

The **Bhagavad Gita** (Sanskrit: भगवद्गीता, lit. "The Song by God"), often referred to as the Gita, is a 700-verse Hindu scripture that is part of the epic *Mahabharata* (Bhishma Parva, chapters 23–40).

It is a dialogue between the Pandava prince **Arjuna** and his guide and charioteer **Krishna** (an avatar of Lord Vishnu).

## The 18 Yogas
The Gita is divided into 18 chapters, each named as a specific "Yoga" or path of connection with the Divine.

1.  **Arjuna Vishada Yoga:** The Yoga of Arjuna's Dejection.
2.  **Sankhya Yoga:** The Yoga of Analysis and Knowledge.
3.  **Karma Yoga:** The Yoga of Action.
4.  **Jnana-Karma-Sanyasa Yoga:** The Yoga of Renunciation of Action in Knowledge.
5.  **Karma-Sanyasa Yoga:** The Yoga of Renunciation.
6.  **Dhyana Yoga:** The Yoga of Meditation (Atma Samyama).
7.  **Jnana-Vijnana Yoga:** The Yoga of Wisdom and Realization.
8.  **Akshara-Brahma Yoga:** The Yoga of the Imperishable Brahman.
9.  **Raja-Vidya-Raja-Guhya Yoga:** The Yoga of the King of Sciences and the King of Secrets.
10. **Vibhuti Yoga:** The Yoga of Divine Glories.
11. **Vishvarupa-Darshana Yoga:** The Yoga of the Vision of the Universal Form.
12. **Bhakti Yoga:** The Yoga of Devotion.
13. **Kshetra-Kshetrajna-Vibhaga Yoga:** The Yoga of Distinction between the Field and the Knower.
14. **Gunatraya-Vibhaga Yoga:** The Yoga of the Three Modes of Material Nature.
15. **Purushottama Yoga:** The Yoga of the Supreme Divine Personality.
16. **Daivasura-Sampad-Vibhaga Yoga:** The Yoga of Distinction between Divine and Demoniac Natures.
17. **Shraddhatraya-Vibhaga Yoga:** The Yoga of the Threefold Division of Faith.
18. **Moksha-Sanyasa Yoga:** The Yoga of Liberation and Renunciation.
