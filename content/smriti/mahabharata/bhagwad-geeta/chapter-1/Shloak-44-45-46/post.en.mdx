# The Surrender to Grief

These three verses mark the climax of Chapter 1. 
1.  **Verse 44:** Arjuna exclaims **"Aho Bata"** (Alas!), realizing that their greed for the kingdom is driving them to commit a terrible sin.
2.  **Verse 45:** He decides that non-resistance is better than bloodshed. He is willing to die unarmed.
3.  **Verse 46:** The perspective shifts back to **Sanjaya**. He describes Arjuna casting aside his bow (*Gandiva*) and sitting down, completely overwhelmed by sorrow (`Shoka-samvigna-manasah`).

## Description
**1.44 – 1.46**

"Alas! What madness drives us on?<br />
Where has our noble reason gone?<br />
For greed of throne and royal bed,<br />
We paint the earth a river red.

Let them come with sword and spear,<br />
I shall not strike, I shall not fear.<br />
If they strike me down unarmed,<br />
My soul at least remains unharmed.

Better to die a victim’s death,<br />
Than breathe a murderer’s living breath."

And having spoken words of woe,<br />
He dropped the arrows and the bow.<br />
The warrior spirit broke and fled,<br />
As silence settled on the dead.

He sank upon the chariot seat,<br />
Admitting moral, deep defeat.<br />
His heart by heavy grief oppressed,<br />
Seeking in vain for peace and rest.
