# Introduction

The Rigveda, the oldest scripture of mankind, begins with the adoration of **Agni**. This Suktam (1.1) was envisioned by Rishi Madhuchhanda and invokes that supreme light which guides our spiritual journey.

The deity of this hymn is **Agni** and the meter is **Gayatri**.

---

## The First Mantra

> Om Agnim ile purohitam Yajnasya devam rtvijam |
> Hotaram ratnadhatamam || 1 ||

**Transliteration:**
*Om Agnim ile purohitam Yajnasya devam rtvijam*
*Hotaram ratnadhatamam*

---

## Translation

I glorify **Agni** (ile), who is:

1.  **Purohitam:** The High Priest of the sacrifice (who leads us forward).
2.  **Yajnasya Devam:** The divine illuminator of the Yajna.
3.  **Rtvijam:** The divine ministrant who performs the rite at the right time.
4.  **Hotaram:** The Summoner (who calls the gods to us).
5.  **Ratnadhatamam:** The greatest holder of gems (spiritual and material wealth).

---

## Commentary

This mantra speaks not just of the physical fire, but of the **Spiritual Fire** (the flame of consciousness).

### 1. Purohit
The word 'Purohit' means *Pura-Hitam* (placed in the front). Agni is placed first in every auspicious act. Just as a guide shows us the path, Agni elevates our consciousness.

### 2. Ratnadhatamam
This is the most beautiful epithet of Agni. Agni is the bestower of **gems**.
* **Materially:** Health, wealth, and prosperity.
* **Spiritually:** The gems of wisdom, discrimination, and self-realization.
